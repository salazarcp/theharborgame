## News #1 — Yayına hazır İngilizce taslak

### What Is Harbor? One Safe Zone. Two Critical Resources.

Harbor is CastilvaGames’ competitive first-person, post-apocalyptic open-world survival game for Windows PC. It brings online PvP and co-op into a wasteland where resources are scarce, death can cost everything you carry, and one place offers temporary safety: Harbor.

The shortest way to understand the game is **one safe zone, two critical resources, and two moving resource targets**.

### One safe zone

Harbor is the only safe zone in the game world. It is where survivors can accept missions, trade resources, restock, and gain temporary protection before returning to the open world. Outside its gates, cooperation is a choice rather than a guarantee. Players can form alliances and trade, but the same world also supports online PvP.

### Two critical resources

Clean Blood and Fuel sit at the center of survival. Hunger and thirst still matter, but these two resources connect many of the decisions players make across the wasteland. Fuel affects how far you can move and whether you can pursue valuable opportunities. Clean Blood is tied to surviving the hostile conditions beyond Harbor.

We are not publishing final balance values yet. Costs, rates, recovery rules, and server settings can change during development. What is already central is the pressure created by scarcity: every trip must be worth the risk.

### Build, trade, cooperate, or fight

Players scavenge for limited resources, craft tools, weapons, clothing, and other essentials, then build and upgrade shelters. Workbenches and a planned three-stage structure upgrade system support territory development and defense. The same resources can also be traded, creating decisions between immediate survival and longer-term security.

Death has serious consequences. The gear and resources carried into the wasteland can be lost, so returning to Harbor—or taking one more risk—matters.

### Two moving resource targets

The War Truck and Blackrail are moving sources of valuable resources. Reaching them requires transport and fuel, turning movement into part of the resource economy. We will share a dedicated look at these targets in a later development update.

### Planned for 2026

Harbor is planned for Windows PC in 2026. The Steam page is live and lists Early Access; an Epic Games Store release is also planned, with its public product page coming later. We have not announced an exact day, month, or launch price.
